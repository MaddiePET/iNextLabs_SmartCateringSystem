THEME: Japanese Fusion

COOKING RULES:
No Mirin.
No Sake.
No alcohol-based marinades.
Use rice vinegar + sugar for sweetness and acidity.

SIGNATURE FLAVORS:
Umami, sesame, soy, ginger, nori, miso, matcha.

AUTHENTICITY:
Focus on minimal plating, balanced presentation, and seasonal ingredients.

SIGNATURE DISH CONCEPTS:
Vegetable Sushi,
Mushroom Teriyaki,
Agedashi Tofu,
Edamame,
Matcha Desserts.

SUBSTITUTIONS:
Use tamari for gluten-free menus.
Use vegan mayo and coconut milk alternatives for dairy-free menus.

COURSE FLOW:
A complete Japanese fusion catering menu should include:
- balanced protein dish
- rice or noodle component
- fresh or chilled element
- umami-focused side dish
- lighter vegetable balance
- clean dessert finish

MENU BALANCE:

Balance:
- hot and cold dishes
- crispy and soft textures
- savory and refreshing flavors

Avoid menus that:
- rely excessively on fried items
- feel too carb-heavy
- repeat soy-based flavors excessively.

PREMIUM EXPERIENCE:
Use minimalist plating and seasonal garnish.

PREMIUM PRESENTATION:
Focus on minimalist plating, negative space, seasonal garnish, ceramic presentation, and refined visual balance.

EVENT SUITABILITY:
Suitable for:
- premium weddings
- executive dinners
- cocktail receptions
- minimalist corporate events
- omakase-inspired catering experiences.

SERVICE STYLE:
Best suited for minimalist buffet service, plated executive dining, and omakase-inspired presentation styles.
Maintain separate hot and chilled serving stations.

OPERATIONAL NOTES:
Sushi freshness and temperature control are critical.
Avoid prolonged holding of tempura items.
Rice texture and moisture must be maintained during transport.
Cold dishes should remain visually fresh and cleanly plated.
