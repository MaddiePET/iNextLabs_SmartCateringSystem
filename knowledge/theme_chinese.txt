THEME: Chinese Fusion

COOKING RULES:
No Shaoxing wine or non-halal cooking wine.
Use ginger juice, rice vinegar, or halal-certified soy sauce as substitutes.

SIGNATURE DISH CONCEPTS:
Chicken Fried Rice
Sweet and Sour Fish
Steamed Ginger Fish
Mapo Tofu
Garlic Bok Choy
Mango Sago Dessert

AUTHENTICITY:
Focus on wok hei, glossy sauces, ginger, garlic, scallions, sesame oil, and balanced umami flavors.

PREMIUM PRESENTATION:
Use banquet-style plating and family-style sharing concepts for premium events.

MENU BALANCE:
Avoid:
- repetitive fried items
- overly carb-heavy menus
- excessive sauce-heavy dishes

Balance:
- crispy textures
- vegetables
- seafood/protein
- lighter palate-cleansing items

OPERATIONAL NOTES:
Spring rolls lose crispness quickly.
Fish dishes require careful temperature control during transport.

EVENT SUITABILITY:
Suitable for:
- engagement dinners
- weddings
- festive banquets
- large-scale celebrations

PREMIUM EXPERIENCE:
Consider:
- dim sum platters
- live noodle stations
- tea pairing service
- cold appetizer platters

COURSE FLOW:
A complete Chinese banquet-style catering menu should include:
- hearty protein-based main
- wok-fried or sauce-based dish
- vegetable balance dish
- crispy texture item
- rice or noodle base
- refreshing dessert or fruit-based finish

SERVICE STYLE:
Best suited for banquet-style buffet service and family-style shared dining.
Maintain separate hot holding and cold dessert stations.

BANQUET EXPERIENCE:
Chinese banquet menus should feel abundant, shareable, and progressively layered in richness across the meal.
