THEME: Western Corporate

COOKING RULES: 
Strictly no wine, brandy, or beer used in deglazing or sauces. Use lemon juice, apple cider vinegar, or vegetable stock as substitutes for acidity.

SIGNATURE DISH CONCEPTS:
Herb Roasted Chicken
Pan-Seared Salmon
Mushroom Risotto
Garlic Mashed Potatoes
Caesar Salad
Roasted Seasonal Vegetables
Chocolate Brownie
Pasta Primavera

MENU BALANCE:
Avoid menus that are:
- excessively heavy
- overly cream-based
- too protein-dense
- lacking fresh vegetables or lighter sides

Balance:
- roasted proteins
- fresh salads
- vegetable sides
- creamy and acidic components
- warm and chilled dishes.

SIGNATURE FLAVORS:
Rosemary, thyme, garlic, butter, black pepper, lemon, cream sauces, roasted vegetables, and herb-infused oils.

COURSE FLOW:
A complete Western corporate catering menu should include:
- premium protein main
- lighter salad or appetizer
- vegetable balance side
- starch or carbohydrate component
- elegant dessert finish
- optional beverage pairing

Balance:
- warm and chilled dishes
- rich and refreshing flavors
- crispy and creamy textures.

AUTHENTICITY: 
Focus on plating aesthetics, fresh herbs (rosemary, thyme), and professional presentation suitable for office settings or gala dinners.

SUBSTITUTIONS: 
Use Beef Bacon or Turkey Ham for flavor. Use Halal-certified butter and cream.

EVENT SUITABILITY:
Suitable for:
- corporate lunches
- executive meetings
- gala dinners
- networking events
- formal award ceremonies.

PREMIUM EXPERIENCE:
For gala dinners and executive events:
- use plated presentation
- include elegant garnish consistency
- prioritize clean modern presentation
- consider live carving or pasta stations
- include premium dessert plating.

PREMIUM PRESENTATION:
Focus on clean plating, structured portions, balanced plate colors, and modern presentation aesthetics.
Use garnish consistency and professional plating standards.

CORPORATE DINING EXPECTATIONS:
Menus should feel professional, refined, and easy to consume during networking or formal business events.
Avoid messy, overly spicy, or difficult-to-handle dishes for networking and executive events.

OPERATIONAL NOTES:
Steak and roasted proteins lose quality if overheld.
Cold salads require chilled transport.
Cream-based sauces may separate during prolonged holding.
Fried items lose crispness during transport.
Plated meals require careful timing coordination for premium presentation.
