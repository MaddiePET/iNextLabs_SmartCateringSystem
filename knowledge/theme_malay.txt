THEME: Traditional Malay

COOKING RULES: 
Naturally Halal focus. No Pork. No Lard. 

SIGNATURE DISH CONCEPTS:
Chicken Satay
Beef Rendang
Nasi Lemak
Ayam Percik
Sayur Goreng
Sambal Udang
Kuih-Muih Platters
Pandan Coconut Desserts

SIGNATURE FLAVORS:
Lemongrass, turmeric, pandan, coconut milk, chili, tamarind, galangal, kaffir lime, and roasted spices.

SUBSTITUTIONS: 
Use Spiced Roasted Chickpea for Nut-Free Satay sauce. Use Coconut Oil instead of Ghee for Vegan.

AUTHENTICITY:
Focus on rempah layering, coconut richness, lemongrass, turmeric, and slow-cooked flavors.

EVENT SUITABILITY:
Suitable for kenduri, weddings, Hari Raya, and communal dining events.

MENU BALANCE:
Avoid menus that are:
- overly coconut-heavy
- excessively spicy
- too oily
- lacking fresh vegetables or acidic side dishes.

Balance:
- spicy sambals
- rich curries
- fresh herbs
- crispy textures
- lighter vegetable dishes
- refreshing desserts.

OPERATIONAL NOTES:
Coconut-based dishes spoil faster in outdoor heat.
Fried items lose crispness quickly during holding.
Sambals should be stored separately during transport to preserve texture.
Rice dishes require proper hot holding to prevent drying.

PREMIUM PRESENTATION:
Use elegant buffet presentation with brass servingware, banana leaf accents, and garnish consistency.
Focus on rich colors:
- deep red sambals
- golden turmeric tones
- green herbs and cucumber garnishes.

PREMIUM EXPERIENCE:
For premium weddings and corporate events:
- include carving stations
- include premium rendang presentation
- include layered dessert platters
- include live beverage stations.

COMMUNAL DINING STYLE:
Malay catering is best suited for communal buffet dining and kenduri-style serving.
Menus should feel generous, comforting, and suitable for mixed age groups.

FESTIVE EXPERIENCE:
For weddings and Hari Raya events:
- include live satay stations
- include kuih assortments
- include refreshing drinks such as sirap bandung or teh ais
- prioritize generous buffet flow and fast serving.

SERVICE STYLE:
Best suited for buffet-style service and large communal events.
Maintain separate hot holding for curries and grilled items.

COURSE FLOW:
A complete Malay catering menu should include:
- rich protein dish
- spicy sambal element
- vegetable balance
- rice or starch base
- refreshing dessert or beverage
- lighter palate-balancing side dish.

GUEST EXPERIENCE:
Balance spice intensity for mixed-age-group events and corporate audiences.
Avoid excessively spicy menus for large-scale public catering.
